# What to Bring on PRC Board Exam Day Philippines (Complete Checklist 2026)

*By [Author Name], [Credential] | Last Updated: April 2026 | 6-minute read*

---

You have been reviewing for months. You know your material. The last thing you want is to get turned away at the exam venue door because of a missing document or the wrong type of ID.

It sounds extreme, but it happens. Every exam cycle, some examinees are denied entry because they forgot their Notice of Admission or brought an expired ID. This guide makes sure that does not happen to you.

## The Non-Negotiables: What You Absolutely Cannot Leave Without

These two items are required for entry to every PRC board exam. Without both of them, you will not be allowed inside the examination room.

**1. Your Printed Notice of Admission (NOA)**

This is the document you downloaded from your LERIS account after PRC approved your application. It contains your exam room assignment, seat number, and personal details.

It must be:
- Printed on white bond paper (A4 or short bond)
- Clear and readable, not faded
- Physical printout, not a screenshot on your phone

Some examinees try to show their NOA on their phone screen. Proctors do not accept this. Print it.

**2. One Valid Government-Issued ID with Photo**

PRC accepts these IDs:
- Passport
- Driver's License
- PhilSys National ID
- UMID (SSS or GSIS)
- PRC ID (if you are a licensed professional in another field)
- Voter's ID
- Postal ID
- NBI Clearance with photo
- School ID (only for currently enrolled students, with school's dry seal and registrar's signature)

Your ID must be original. Photocopies are not accepted. Expired IDs are not accepted.

---

## What Else to Bring

These items are not required for entry but are highly recommended:

**Pencils (No. 2 pencils only):**
Most PRC board exams still use paper answer sheets that require No. 2 or HB pencils. Bring at least 3 to 4 sharpened pencils. Do not rely on only one.

**A good pencil sharpener:**
Not the hand-held kind. Bring a small manual sharpener or a battery-operated one. Broken tips mid-exam are a real annoyance.

**An eraser (white, clean, non-smudging):**
Use a good quality eraser that removes marks cleanly without leaving smudges on the answer sheet. Poor erasure can confuse the scanning machine and cost you points.

**A watch:**
Your phone must be turned off or surrendered at the door at most PRC testing centers. A simple analog or digital watch lets you manage your time during the exam. Smart watches are generally not allowed.

**Water and a small snack:**
Some testing centers allow water bottles (sealed, clear containers only). Confirm this with your specific venue. A light snack for the break between exam parts keeps your energy up.

**Cash for emergencies:**
For transportation, food, or any unexpected situation. Do not bring large amounts.

## What NOT to Bring

PRC and most testing centers prohibit these items. Bringing them can get you disqualified:

- Mobile phones (must be surrendered or left outside the room)
- Smart watches of any kind
- Earphones or any listening device
- Calculators (unless your specific exam allows it, such as some engineering boards)
- Notes, review materials, or flashcards
- Bags larger than a small pouch (in some venues, all bags must be left outside)
- Food inside the exam room (unless medically necessary with documentation)

When in doubt about a specific item, check the rules posted on your Notice of Admission or on the official PRC website for your specific board exam.

---

## What to Do the Night Before

The night before your exam is not the time to squeeze in extra reviewer material. It is the time to prepare physically and logistically.

**Do these the night before:**
- Lay out all your items: NOA, ID, pencils, eraser, sharpener, watch
- Check your room assignment on your NOA
- Look up your testing center on Google Maps and note how long it takes to get there
- Set your alarm at least 90 minutes earlier than you think you need
- Sleep at a reasonable time. Aim for 7 to 8 hours.

**Do not do these the night before:**
- Start reviewing a new topic you have not studied before
- Stay up past midnight cramming
- Eat anything that might upset your stomach on exam day

---

## What Time Should You Arrive?

PRC board exam venues typically require examinees to be seated by a specific time stated on the NOA. Latecomers are not allowed to enter after the exam begins.

The general rule is: aim to arrive at least 45 minutes to 1 hour before the official start time. This gives you time to:
- Find the specific building and room (especially at large venues)
- Settle into your seat
- Use the restroom before the exam begins
- Calm your nerves without rushing

For exams held in large venues like universities with multiple buildings, give yourself even more buffer time. Getting lost on exam day is more common than people think.

## PRC Board Exam Day Checklist (Print-Friendly)

| Item | Required | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Printed Notice of Admission (NOA) | YES | Physical printout only. No phone screenshots. |
| Valid Government-Issued ID (original) | YES | Not expired. Not a photocopy. |
| No. 2 or HB Pencils (3 to 4 pieces) | Recommended | Sharpened and ready. |
| Pencil Sharpener | Recommended | Small manual or battery type. |
| White Eraser | Recommended | Non-smudging. |
| Analog or Digital Watch | Recommended | Smart watches NOT allowed. |
| Sealed Water Bottle (clear) | Optional | Confirm allowance at your specific venue. |
| Small Snack for Break | Optional | Check venue rules before bringing inside. |
| Cash | Recommended | For transportation and emergencies. |
| Mobile Phone | NO | Will be surrendered or prohibited inside. |
| Smart Watch | NO | Not allowed in exam rooms. |
| Review Materials | NO | Not allowed inside the exam room. |

*Rules may vary slightly by testing center and profession. Always read your NOA carefully for venue-specific reminders.*
