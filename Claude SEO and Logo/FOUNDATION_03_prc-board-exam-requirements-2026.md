# PRC Board Exam Requirements 2026 Philippines (Complete Document List)

*By [Author Name], [Credential] | Last Updated: April 2026 | 8-minute read*

---

Getting your documents wrong is one of the most avoidable reasons people delay their board exam. You studied for months, finally feel ready, start the application, and then realize you need to get a document fixed or re-requested from your school.

This guide covers what you need to prepare for the PRC board exam application in 2026, including the documents required by almost every profession and the additional ones specific to particular boards.

One important reminder before we dive in: always verify the exact requirements for your specific profession directly at the PRC website (prc.gov.ph) or through your PRC regional office. Requirements can change between exam cycles.

## General Requirements (Almost Every PRC Board Exam)

These documents are required across most PRC licensure examinations. Prepare all of them regardless of which profession you are applying for.

### 1. Printed Application Form

You fill this out through the PRC LERIS portal at online.prc.gov.ph. Once submitted and approved, you print it and bring it to your document verification appointment.

### 2. PSA-Authenticated Birth Certificate

This must be from the Philippine Statistics Authority, not just a Local Civil Registry copy. If you do not have a PSA copy, order one at serbilis.psa.gov.ph or through a PSA outlet or authorized courier service.

Ordering PSA documents takes time. If you do not have yours yet, request it now, not a week before the deadline.

### 3. Official Transcript of Records (OTR) with School Dry Seal

Your OTR must be issued directly by your college or university with the registrar's signature and school dry seal. Do not submit your own copy or a photocopy. Some schools require 2 to 3 weeks to process OTRs so request this early.

### 4. Certificate of Registration or Diploma

Some boards require a photocopy of your diploma while others require a Certificate of Graduation issued by your school's registrar. Check your specific profession's requirements.

### 5. Recent 2x2 ID Photos

PRC has strict photo specifications. The photo must be:
- Taken within the last 3 months
- On a white background
- In formal attire
- No eyeglasses
- No accessories that cover the face

Bring the required number of copies as stated in your application form. Some applications require 2 to 4 copies.

### 6. Valid Government-Issued ID (with photocopy)

Accepted IDs include:
- Philippine Passport
- Driver's License
- PhilSys National ID
- UMID Card (SSS or GSIS)
- Voter's ID
- Postal ID
- NBI Clearance with photo

Bring the original and one photocopy of both the front and back.

## Additional Requirements by Profession

Beyond the general documents, each profession has specific requirements. Here are the six professions covered on LisensyaPrep.

### Nursing (PNLE)

The PRC Board of Nursing typically requires:
- OTR with school dry seal (for Bachelor of Science in Nursing)
- Certificate of Good Moral Character from your school
- Medical Certificate from a licensed physician (some cycles require this)
- NSO or PSA Birth Certificate

### Criminology (CLE)

The PRC Board of Criminology typically requires:
- OTR for Bachelor of Science in Criminology
- Certificate of Good Moral Character
- Affidavit of No Derogatory Record (some cycles)
- PSA Birth Certificate

### Education (LET)

The PRC Board for Professional Teachers typically requires:
- OTR for a Bachelor's degree in Education or a related field
- Certificate of Good Moral Character
- PSA Birth Certificate
- Certificate of completion for required units if your degree is non-education but with education units

### Pharmacy (PLE)

The PRC Board of Pharmacy typically requires:
- OTR for Bachelor of Science in Pharmacy
- Certificate of Good Moral Character
- PSA Birth Certificate

### Medical Technology (MTLE)

The PRC Board of Medical Technology typically requires:
- OTR for Bachelor of Science in Medical Technology
- Certificate of Good Moral Character
- PSA Birth Certificate

### Agriculture (ALE)

The PRC Board of Agriculture typically requires:
- OTR for Bachelor of Science in Agriculture or related field
- Certificate of Good Moral Character
- PSA Birth Certificate

## How to Prepare Your Documents Without Stress

The biggest mistake applicants make is starting the document collection too late. Here is a realistic timeline for getting everything ready.

**8 to 12 weeks before the application deadline:**
Request your OTR from your school's registrar. University processing can take 2 to 4 weeks. Some schools are even slower. The earlier you request, the better.

**6 to 8 weeks before the deadline:**
Order your PSA Birth Certificate if you do not have a certified copy. Online ordering via the PSA Serbilis portal usually delivers within 2 to 4 weeks.

**4 to 6 weeks before the deadline:**
Have your ID photos taken. Visit a photo studio and specify that the photos are for a PRC board exam application. They will know the exact specs.

**2 to 4 weeks before the deadline:**
Gather all other documents. Check each one against the PRC requirements for your specific profession. Make a checklist and tick off each item.

**1 to 2 weeks before the deadline:**
Submit your online application via LERIS and book your document verification appointment at your nearest PRC office.

## Frequently Asked Questions About PRC Requirements

**Can I submit photocopies instead of originals?**
No. PRC requires original documents at your verification appointment. Bring originals and photocopies. The photocopies are kept by PRC while originals are returned to you.

**What if my name is spelled differently on my OTR and my Birth Certificate?**
This needs to be corrected before you apply. A discrepancy in name spelling can cause your application to be rejected. Contact your school registrar to have the OTR corrected, or file a petition with the PSA if the error is on your birth certificate.

**My school closed down. How do I get my OTR?**
Contact CHED (Commission on Higher Education) for guidance. They maintain records of closed schools and can help you obtain certified copies of your academic records.

**Is a Good Moral Character certificate required?**
For most professions, yes. This is usually issued by your school's guidance office or registrar. Some professions also accept a Good Moral certificate from your barangay or church. Check your specific board's requirements.
