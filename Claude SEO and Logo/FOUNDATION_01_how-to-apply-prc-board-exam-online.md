# How to Apply for PRC Board Exam Online 2026 (Step by Step via LERIS)

*By [Author Name], [Credential] | Last Updated: April 2026 | 8-minute read*

---

Filing your PRC board exam application should be the easy part of your board exam journey. And honestly, it is easy once you know what to expect. The problem is that a lot of first-time applicants find themselves confused by the LERIS portal, unsure which documents to prepare, or worried they missed a deadline.

This guide walks you through the entire process from start to finish. By the time you finish reading, you will know exactly what to do, what to prepare, and what to watch out for.

## What is PRC LERIS?

LERIS stands for Licensure Examination and Registration Information System. It is the official online portal of the Professional Regulation Commission where all board exam applicants submit their applications.

Before LERIS existed, applicants had to fall in line at their nearest PRC office just to apply. Now you can do almost everything online, which is a big deal especially for applicants from provinces far from PRC regional offices.

The LERIS portal is at: **online.prc.gov.ph**

Bookmark that link. Everything happens there.

---

## What You Need to Prepare Before You Start

Do not start your online application without these ready. Getting halfway through and realizing you are missing a document is frustrating and wastes time.

**Documents to scan or photograph:**
- Recent ID photo (2x2, white background, taken within the last 3 months)
- PSA Birth Certificate
- Certificate of Registration/Diploma from your school
- Official Transcript of Records (OTR) from your university
- Any additional document required by your specific profession (check PRC website per board)

**Photo specifications PRC requires:**
- Size: 2x2 inches
- Background: Plain white only
- Taken within the last 3 months
- Formal attire
- No eyeglasses
- JPEG or PNG format, under 2MB file size

If your photo does not meet these specs, your application will be flagged and you will need to resubmit.

## Step-by-Step: How to Apply via PRC LERIS

### Step 1: Create Your LERIS Account

Go to online.prc.gov.ph and click "Create Account." You will need a valid email address and a password. Use an email you check regularly because PRC sends important notifications there.

Fill in your personal information exactly as it appears on your PSA Birth Certificate. Any mismatch between your application details and your official documents can cause problems during verification, so be precise.

Once you submit, check your email for the verification link and click it to activate your account.

### Step 2: Log In and Fill Out the Application Form

After verifying your email, log in to your LERIS account. Go to "Examination Application" and select the specific board exam you are applying for.

Fill out all required fields. This includes:
- Personal information
- School and degree details
- Contact information
- Emergency contact

Take your time here. Double-check everything before moving to the next screen because some fields cannot be edited after submission.

### Step 3: Upload Your Documents and Photo

You will be asked to upload your 2x2 ID photo and any required supporting documents. Make sure your files are clear, properly cropped, and within the file size limits the system specifies.

For the photo upload, LERIS has a built-in cropping tool. Use it to make sure your face is properly framed within the photo box.

### Step 4: Choose Your Exam Schedule and Testing Center

LERIS will show you the available exam dates and testing centers for your chosen board exam. Slots are limited and fill up quickly, especially for popular professions like Nursing, Criminology, and Teaching.

A few practical tips here:
- Apply as early as PRC opens the application window for your exam cycle
- If your preferred testing center is full, check other centers nearby
- NCR centers fill up fastest, so provincial applicants sometimes have better luck with their local PRC testing center

### Step 5: Pay the Application Fee

After choosing your slot, you will be directed to the payment page. PRC accepts payment through several channels including over-the-counter bank payments and some online payment options.

Save your payment receipt. You will need to present it during document verification. The fee is non-refundable even if you miss the exam.

Check the current examination fee on the official PRC website since rates are updated periodically.

### Step 6: Book Your Appointment for Document Verification

Even though the application is mostly online, you still need to appear physically at a PRC office to submit your original documents and have them verified. Book your appointment slot through LERIS after paying.

Bring all original documents plus photocopies to your appointment. Originals are returned to you after verification. Photocopies are kept by PRC.

### Step 7: Download Your Notice of Admission

Once PRC approves your application, you can download your Notice of Admission from your LERIS account. Print this and keep it safe. You are required to bring it to the exam venue on exam day.

## Important Deadlines to Know

PRC sets application deadlines for each exam cycle and they do not give extensions. Missing the deadline means waiting for the next cycle, which is usually six months away.

Here is how to stay on top of deadlines:

- Follow the official PRC Facebook page for announcements
- Check prc.gov.ph regularly in the months before your target exam cycle
- Set a reminder on your phone for the application opening date, not just the deadline

The application window typically opens about 2 to 3 months before the exam date, depending on the profession.

---

## Common Application Mistakes to Avoid

**Using a personal nickname instead of your legal name:** Your application must match your PSA documents exactly. Even a missing middle initial can cause issues.

**Submitting a photo that does not meet specs:** This is the most common reason for flagged applications. When in doubt, have your photo taken at a professional photo studio and tell them it is for a PRC application.

**Waiting until the last week to apply:** Slots for popular testing centers run out fast. Apply as early as the window opens.

**Ignoring LERIS email notifications:** PRC sends updates about your application status through email. Check your inbox and spam folder regularly.
