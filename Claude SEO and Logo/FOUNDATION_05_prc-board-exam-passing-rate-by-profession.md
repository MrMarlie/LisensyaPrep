# PRC Board Exam Passing Rate by Profession 2025 to 2026 (Updated)

*By [Author Name], [Credential] | Last Updated: April 2026 | 7-minute read*

---

Before you start your review, it helps to know what you are actually up against. Passing rates vary wildly between PRC board exams. Some professions have consistently high passing rates above 80%. Others sit at 30 to 40%, which means more than half of all examinees do not make it on any given cycle.

This page tracks the latest passing rates across six major PRC professions. We update it after every exam cycle using official results announced by the Professional Regulation Commission.

Use this data not to scare yourself but to calibrate your preparation. A lower passing rate does not mean the exam is impossible. It means more people who take it are underprepared. That is entirely avoidable.

## Latest PRC Passing Rates by Profession

### Nursing (PNLE / NLE)

**February 2026 Results:**
- Total Examinees: 8,162
- Total Passers: 3,611
- Passing Rate: 44.24%

The NLE consistently has one of the lower passing rates among major PRC boards. This is not because the exam is impossibly hard but because it attracts a very high volume of examinees, many of whom take it without completing a structured review.

The good news: examinees who follow a disciplined review plan consistently perform above the national average. The 44% figure includes everyone who sat for the exam, including those who reviewed for only a few weeks.

### Criminology (CLE)

**February 2026 Results:**
- Total Examinees: 45,936
- Total Passers: 30,320
- Passing Rate: 65.99%

The CLE has one of the largest examinee pools of any PRC board, with nearly 46,000 people taking the exam in a single cycle. The passing rate hovers in the mid-60s range, which is respectable.

The volume means that even small improvements in your preparation quality can meaningfully improve your chances. The bar is not impossibly high. It rewards people who actually review.

### Medical Technology (MTLE)

**February 2026 Results:**
- Total Examinees: 9,317
- Total Passers: 7,838
- Passing Rate: 84.12%

Medical Technology consistently produces the highest passing rate among the six professions on LisensyaPrep. An 84% passing rate means that a well-prepared examinee has a very strong chance of passing on the first attempt.

The smaller examinee pool compared to Nursing and Criminology also reflects the more specialized nature of the program.

### Education (LET)

The LET has a historically variable passing rate. It tends to sit between 30% and 50% depending on the cycle. The elementary level generally has a slightly higher passing rate than the secondary level.

For the most current LET passing rate, check the official PRC results announcement at prc.gov.ph after each September and March cycle. We update this page within one week of each official release.

### Pharmacy (PLE)

Pharmacy passing rates typically fall in the 55% to 75% range depending on the cycle. The exam rewards structured subject-by-subject preparation because its coverage spans very different fields: pharmacology, chemistry, law, and clinical practice.

Check the latest PLE results at prc.gov.ph and on this page after each exam cycle.

### Agriculture (ALE)

Agriculture passing rates have historically varied widely, sometimes as low as 30% and as high as 70% depending on the cycle and the specific curriculum batch taking the exam.

The wide variance makes consistent structured review even more important for ALE takers. We update this page after each cycle with official PRC data.

## What These Numbers Actually Mean for You

A passing rate of 44% like the NLE sounds discouraging until you look at it from a different angle. Nearly half of the people who sit for that exam pass. Among those who consistently reviewed for 3 months or more with structured materials, the pass rate is almost certainly much higher.

Passing rates are pulled down by examinees who:
- Took the exam without reviewing seriously
- Reviewed in an unstructured way without covering all subjects
- Had strong subjects but neglected weak areas until it was too late
- Struggled with time management during the actual exam

None of those factors apply to someone following a structured review plan. If you use LisensyaPrep's subject reviewers, follow a real study schedule, and practice with mock exams, you are not competing against the full pool of examinees. You are competing against the prepared ones.

---

## How Passing Rates Are Calculated

PRC uses a simple formula:

**Passing Rate (%) = (Number of Passers / Total Examinees) x 100**

The "passers" count only includes examinees who met both of the required thresholds:
- A general weighted average of at least 75%
- No individual subject score below the minimum (usually 60%)

Both conditions must be met. This is why some examinees end up with high averages but still do not pass because one weak subject pulled below the minimum threshold.

This is also why subject-by-subject review matters so much. Ignoring any single subject is a real risk.

## Checking Historical Passing Rates

The PRC website maintains results going back many years. If you want to see passing rate trends for your specific profession over multiple cycles, visit prc.gov.ph and go to the Board Results section for your profession.

Looking at historical data helps you:
- See whether a profession's passing rate is trending up or down
- Understand which exam cycles tend to be harder or easier
- Set a realistic target score based on what typical passers achieve
