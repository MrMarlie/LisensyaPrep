# How to Check PRC Board Exam Results Online 2026 (Step by Step Guide)

*By [Author Name], [Credential] | Last Updated: April 2026 | 6-minute read*

---

The days after a PRC board exam are some of the longest of your life. You finished the exam, you walked out hoping for the best, and now you are refreshing the PRC website every few hours trying to find out if your name is on the list.

This guide tells you exactly where to check, when results typically come out, and what the different result scenarios mean for you.

## Where to Check PRC Board Exam Results

PRC posts results in multiple places. Here are the official sources, ranked by reliability.

**1. Official PRC Website: prc.gov.ph**

This is the most authoritative source. Go to prc.gov.ph, look for the "Exam Results" section in the navigation or main page, and find your specific board exam.

PRC typically posts a PDF or searchable list of passers by surname. You can use Ctrl+F (or Command+F on Mac) to search for your name in the document.

**2. PRC LERIS Portal: online.prc.gov.ph**

After results are released, you can also check your individual rating (your actual score) through your LERIS account. Log in, go to "Verification of Rating," and enter your details.

This is particularly useful because it shows your individual subject scores, not just whether you passed.

**3. Official PRC Social Media Pages**

PRC Philippines often announces results through their official Facebook page. This is usually where word spreads first, but always verify at the official website because unofficial announcements and screenshots circulate quickly and are sometimes inaccurate or premature.

**Important:** Do not rely on third-party websites, Facebook groups, or Telegram channels as your primary source. Results from unofficial sources can be delayed, incomplete, or wrong.

## When Do PRC Results Come Out?

Every board exam has a different results release timeline. Here are the standard release schedules for the six professions on LisensyaPrep.

| Board Exam | Typical Results Release |
|------------|------------------------|
| Nursing (PNLE) | Within 5 working days after last exam day |
| Criminology (CLE) | Within 5 to 10 working days |
| Education (LET) | Up to 60 working days (this one takes long) |
| Pharmacy (PLE) | Within 5 to 10 working days |
| Medical Technology (MTLE) | Within 3 to 5 working days |
| Agriculture (ALE) | Within 5 to 10 working days |

These are typical timelines based on historical PRC release patterns. The actual release date for each cycle may vary. PRC does not always announce in advance exactly when results will drop, so checking regularly is part of the waiting process.

The LET results take significantly longer than other boards because of the sheer volume of examinees. Do not panic if LET results take 2 to 3 months.

## How to Check Your Rating (Individual Score)

Finding your name on the passers list tells you that you passed. But if you want to see your actual score per subject, you do that through the PRC online rating verification system.

Here is how:

**Step 1:** Go to online.prc.gov.ph and log in to your LERIS account.

**Step 2:** Click on "Verification of Rating" in the menu.

**Step 3:** Enter the required details (your exam type, exam date, and personal information).

**Step 4:** Your rating breakdown will appear, showing your score per subject and your general weighted average.

This is especially useful if you are a passer who wants to see exactly how you performed per subject, or if you did not pass and you need to understand which subjects you need to retake.

---

## What Happens If Your Name is NOT on the List?

First: take a breath. Not seeing your name does not automatically mean you failed. It might mean results are being released in batches, or there was a data entry issue, or the PDF has not fully loaded.

Wait for the complete passers list to be officially released before drawing conclusions.

If after the full release your name is genuinely not on the passers list, here is what to do:

**Check your rating through LERIS.** Your individual scores tell you exactly which subjects you passed and which you did not. This is the information you need to plan your next steps.

**Understand the retake policy.** PRC allows you to retake the board exam. Some boards allow you to retake only the subjects you failed rather than the entire exam. This varies by profession so check the specific retake rules for your board.

We have a separate guide on PRC board exam retake policies that covers this in detail.

**Do not make any decisions based on emotion right away.** The first few days after a difficult result feel overwhelming. Give yourself time before deciding your next course of action.

---

## Tips for the Waiting Period

The days between taking the exam and seeing the results are genuinely stressful. A few things that help:

**Set a schedule for when you check.** Constantly refreshing the PRC website every 10 minutes will drive you up the wall. Pick two or three specific times per day to check and otherwise try to get your mind on something else.

**Follow LisensyaPrep and the official PRC page.** We post updates as soon as results are released. So does the PRC Facebook page. Following these saves you from having to constantly check the website manually.

**Trust your preparation.** By the time you are waiting for results, the hard work is done. Nothing you do in the waiting period changes what happened in that exam room. Let yourself rest.

---

## After You Pass: What Comes Next?

Congratulations. Finding your name on that passers list is one of the best feelings you will have in your professional life.

Here is what happens after:

**Oath Taking Ceremony:** PRC schedules oath taking ceremonies for new passers. You register for a slot through your LERIS account. This is where you formally take your oath as a licensed professional.

**Initial Registration:** After oath taking, you apply for your PRC Certificate of Registration and Professional Identification Card (PIC). This is done through LERIS as well.

**PRC License:** Your license and ID card are released after your initial registration is processed. This is the document you will use throughout your professional career.
