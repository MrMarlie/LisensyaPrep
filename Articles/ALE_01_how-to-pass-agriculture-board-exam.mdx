## Why the ALE Has Variable Passing Rates

The Agriculture board exam is known for having one of the widest passing rate fluctuations among all PRC boards. It can swing from below 30 percent to above 70 percent depending on the cycle.

This is not random. The variance reflects two things. First, the examinee pool changes significantly between cycles, with some cycles attracting more first-time takers and others attracting more repeat takers. Second, the exam coverage can shift emphasis between areas, rewarding examinees who reviewed broadly rather than those who focused on only a few subjects.

The practical lesson is this: you cannot predict which subjects will be emphasized in your cycle. The only safe approach is to review all subject areas adequately rather than betting on certain topics.

## 6 Tips That Actually Help You Pass the ALE

### 1. Review Broadly, Not Deep on One Subject

Because the ALE passing rate varies so widely between cycles, the safest strategy is broad coverage across all five subject areas rather than deep mastery of two or three. A score of 70 percent across all subjects beats a 90 percent in two subjects and a 55 percent in the others.

### 2. Know Your Philippine-Specific Content

The ALE frequently emphasizes Philippine agricultural context. Know the major crop varieties grown in the Philippines (the NSIC-registered rice varieties, the major commercial vegetable crops, the dominant livestock breeds). Generic textbook knowledge is good, but Philippine-specific applications earn the points.

### 3. Master Soil pH and Nutrient Relationships

If there is one topic that appears in virtually every ALE cycle it is soil pH and its effect on nutrient availability. The relationship between pH and which nutrients become available or locked in the soil is foundational. Know that most nutrients are available between pH 6.0 and 7.0, that acidic soils cause aluminum and manganese toxicity, and that alkaline soils cause deficiencies in iron, manganese, zinc, and copper.

### 4. Practice Agricultural Calculations

Farm budgeting, fertilizer rate calculations, break-even analysis, and return on investment are calculation-based questions that many examinees skip during review because they feel like math. Do not skip them. They are predictable and prepare well.

### 5. Use the LisensyaPrep Agriculture Quiz

The Agriculture quiz on LisensyaPrep covers all five subject areas with questions organized by module. Use it both for diagnostic testing at the start of your review and for practice throughout. No account needed.

### 6. Rest the Week Before

ALE examinees who try to cram in the final week consistently report blanking out on questions they knew during review. The brain consolidates knowledge during sleep. Protect your sleep in the final 7 days. Review lightly, rest fully.

## Philippine Agriculture: Key Facts Every ALE Examinee Should Know

**Top crops by area harvested:** Rice, corn, coconut, sugarcane, banana.

**NSIC:** The National Seed Industry Council registers approved plant varieties in the Philippines. PRC-registered rice varieties like NSIC Rc series appear in ALE questions.

**DA and attached agencies:** Department of Agriculture, PhilRice (Philippine Rice Research Institute), BAR (Bureau of Agricultural Research), PCAMRD, and others appear in agricultural economics and extension questions.

**Major livestock in the Philippines:** Cattle, carabao, hog, goat, sheep, poultry (chicken, duck). The carabao is the national animal and is significant in Philippine agriculture for draft power and dairy.
